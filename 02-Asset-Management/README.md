# Phase 2: Asset Management & Classification Matrix

## 📋 Overview
This directory contains the Enterprise Asset Inventory and Classification registry for NexaLearn Inc. In accordance with the **NIST CSF 2.0 Asset Management (AM)** subcategories, an organization cannot protect what it does not know it owns. 

This phase focuses on identifying, labeling, and classifying both tangible and intangible assets. By tying each asset to its primary objective within the **CIA Triad** (Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability) and assigning a business impact rating, we establish a data-driven blueprint for risk prioritization.

---

## 🛠️ Classification Architecture
In alignment with modern enterprise standards, assets are cataloged across five core textbook categories and rated by their operational criticality:
* **Asset Classifications:** Data, Software, Hardware, Network, and Intangible Assets (such as company brand reputation).
* **Security Focus:** Pinpointing whether an asset primarily demands **Confidentiality** (preventing data leaks), **Integrity** (preventing unauthorized tampering), or **Availability** (ensuring 24/7 uptime for global universities).
* **Impact Rating Scale:** Categorizing systems into standardized impact zones (**Very High, High, Moderate**) based on the business, financial, and legal devastation that would result if the asset were compromised.
