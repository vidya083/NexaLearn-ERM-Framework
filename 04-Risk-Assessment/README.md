# Phase 4: Quantitative Risk Assessment Matrix

## 📋 Overview
This directory contains the Quantitative Risk Assessment for NexaLearn Inc. In this phase, we move from identifying abstract threats to calculating empirical risk rankings. By applying a structured mathematical modeling approach ($Risk = Likelihood \times Impact$), we transform qualitative security concerns into an actionable, prioritized roadmap.

This matrix acts as the key decision-making engine for the business, ensuring that our limited cybersecurity resources and budget are allocated to remediate the most critical vulnerabilities first.

---

## 📐 Scoring Methodology
In alignment with enterprise risk management (ERM) best practices and standard cybersecurity frameworks, risks are quantified using a 1–5 scoring scale:

* **Likelihood (1 to 5):** From 1 (Rare/Negligible probability) to 5 (Almost Certain/Frequent exposure).
* **Impact (1 to 5):** From 1 (Low operational or financial inconvenience) to 5 (Catastrophic/Existential damage, such as severe regulatory fines or critical downtime).
* **Risk Score Matrix (1 to 25):** The product of multiplying Likelihood by Impact determines the final criticality score, establishing clear response thresholds:
  * 🔴 **Critical Risk (21–25):** Requires immediate remediation and executive oversight.
  * 🟠 **High Risk (13–20):** Requires scheduled mitigation plans within the current sprint cycle.
  * 🟡 **Medium Risk (6–12):** Monitored with compensating controls applied where feasible.
